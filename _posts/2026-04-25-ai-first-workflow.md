My company recently announced a mandate that 95% of code should be generated by an LLM. Having had about one month in to this new workflow, I wanted to catalog my experiences to date.

## Stages of Grief

It may seem unusual, but moving to an AI-first workflow took me through the stages of grief. Grief is about the pain of loss, and a job change is significant loss. For me, programming is more than just a job, but it's also a hobby and a little bit of my identity.

* Denial - AI isn't good enough; it will never compare to me as a programmer. This is a fad. Tokens are going to be too expensive without VC subsidization, so it won't last. The people using AI are overstating its value and ability.
* Anger - I can't believe they're trying to replace me. Maybe I should just find a different career path. Programming is a joke now. All software is going to fail in 18 months.
* Depression - How will I feed my family once they've totally replaced me? What do I do for fun now that bots will fix everything in open-source? My job is just going to be clicking approve on giant PRs; I'm becoming alienated from the system for which I am responsible.
* Bargaining - I'll burn tokens for non-sense things and maybe just use it for search. Maybe it can write the tests and I'll still write the code.
* Acceptance - This is a bigger picture.

Grief is non-linear, nor is it something that is conquered. There are days where I move from acceptance to anger to denial.
